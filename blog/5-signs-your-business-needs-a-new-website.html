5 Signs Your Business Needs a New Website

Common warning signs that your current website may be costing you enquiries — and what to do about it.

← Back to Blog Published 10 June 2026

Most business owners don’t realise their website is losing them customers until they look at the data.

Your website is often the first impression potential customers have of your business. A modern, professional website can build trust and generate enquiries, while an outdated website can drive visitors away before they ever contact you.

If you’re unsure whether your website is helping or hurting your business, here are five common signs that it may be time for an upgrade.

1. Your website looks outdated

Design trends change quickly. A website that looked modern five years ago may now appear old-fashioned and unprofessional.

Potential customers often form an opinion about a business within seconds of visiting its website. If your website looks dated, visitors may assume your business is too.

A modern website helps build trust, improve credibility and create a better user experience. Take a look at our portfolio to see examples of contemporary business websites.

2. It doesn’t work well on mobile devices

Around two thirds of website traffic in the UK now comes from mobile devices.

If visitors need to zoom in, scroll sideways or struggle to navigate your website on a phone, they are unlikely to stay for long.

A responsive website automatically adapts to different screen sizes, providing a smooth experience for users whether they’re browsing on a phone, tablet or desktop computer.

3. Your website loads slowly

Website speed matters more than many business owners realise.

Imagine a potential customer searching for your service while commuting or sitting at home. They click your website, wait a few seconds and nothing happens. Frustrated, they leave and visit a competitor instead.

Slow-loading websites can reduce enquiries, increase bounce rates and negatively impact search engine rankings.

A fast website provides a better experience for visitors and increases the chances of turning traffic into customers.

4. You’re not getting enquiries

A website’s primary purpose is usually to generate leads, enquiries or sales.

If people are visiting your website but not making contact, the problem may not be your business. It could be your website.

Common issues include:

  • Poor calls-to-action
  • Confusing navigation
  • Outdated design
  • Weak messaging
  • Slow loading speeds

Even small improvements can have a significant impact on conversion rates.

5. It’s difficult to update

Many businesses are stuck with websites that are difficult to manage or rely on outdated technology.

If updating content feels complicated or requires contacting a developer for every small change, your website may be holding your business back.

A modern website should be secure, easy to maintain and capable of growing alongside your business. Our website maintenance services can help if you need ongoing support.

Is it time for a new website?

If any of these signs sound familiar, it may be worth reviewing your current website.
